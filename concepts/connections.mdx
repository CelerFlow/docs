---
title: "Connections"
description: "Team-scoped OAuth grants powered by Composio."
---

A connection is an OAuth grant that lets your workflows act on behalf of your team in an external service — Gmail, Notion, HubSpot, Slack, or Firecrawl. Connections are shared: connect once, and every team member's workflows can use it.

## How connections are scoped

Connections are stored in `team_connections` and are shared across all members of the team. Composio tracks each user's connected account under the entity ID `celerflow-${userId}` for deduplication, but connection membership is team-level. You don't need to reconnect when a colleague joins.

## Connecting a service

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Connections">
    Navigate to your workspace settings and select the Connections tab. See [Settings](/dashboard/settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect">
    Click **Connect** next to the service you want to add. The backend starts a Composio connected account flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete OAuth">
    A popup opens the provider's OAuth consent screen. Authorize access and the popup closes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for confirmation">
    The frontend polls `/oauth/status` with exponential backoff — roughly 2 s → 3 s → 5 s → 8 s → 12 s, up to about 30 seconds total — until Composio reports the account as active. The card updates to **Connected** when done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stale and deleted connections

If Composio returns a `404` for a connection, the UI card collapses to `missing` or `not_connected`. You'll need to reconnect.

<Warning>
  Non-404 transient errors (timeouts, 5xx) do **not** silently disconnect a connection. The UI continues treating it as valid. Only a confirmed 404 triggers the missing state.
</Warning>
