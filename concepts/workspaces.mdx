A workspace is the top-level boundary in CelerFlow. Everything you build — workflows, connections, and run history — lives inside one workspace and is shared with your team.

## What a workspace is

In the database each workspace is a single row in `agent_fleets`, accessed via a `[slug]` segment in the URL (e.g. `/{slug}/dashboard`). The slug is stable; bookmark it and share it with teammates.

A workspace is bound to exactly one team. Every team member sees the same workflows, connections, and run history. Access is controlled by role — see [Teams & roles](/platform/teams-rbac) for details.

## Creating your first workspace

When you sign in for the first time, CelerFlow walks you through a short onboarding flow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a use case">
    Choose the category that best describes what your team automates (research, outreach, reporting, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the workspace">
    Give it a short, memorable name — this becomes your slug.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done">
    Your workspace is ready. The default [platform agents](/concepts/platform-agents) are automatically seeded into your team's agent pool so the Planner can start assembling workflows immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Switching workspaces

Use the workspace picker in the top navigation bar to switch between workspaces. If you belong to multiple teams — say, a personal workspace and a company one — each appears in the picker. Clicking a workspace switches all context: workflows, connections, runs, and settings.

<Note>
  Platform agents are seeded once per workspace at creation time. Each workspace gets its own independent pool.
</Note>

## What's shared inside a workspace

| Resource | Scoped to |
|---|---|
| Workflows | Workspace (team) |
| Connections | Workspace (team) |
| Runs & history | Workspace (team) |
| Roles & membership | Workspace (team) |

For connection details see [Connections](/concepts/connections). For workflow building see [Planner](/concepts/planner).
