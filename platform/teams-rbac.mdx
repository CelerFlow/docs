title: "Teams & roles"
description: "How team membership and role-based access work in CelerFlow."
---

Every workspace is owned by a team. Teams are how CelerFlow shares connections, workflows, and runs between multiple users.

## The four roles

| Role | Level | What it means |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Owner** | 4 | Assigned automatically to the person who creates the team. Full control. |
| **Admin** | 3 | Can invite, remove members, and change roles. |
| **Operator** | 2 | Day-to-day workflow work — build, deploy, rerun, approve. |
| **Viewer** | 1 | Read-only access. |

Roles are hierarchical: a higher role satisfies any check that requires a lower role. Role checks are enforced server-side on every team route — a client that tries to invite without at least the admin role gets a `403`.

## What each role can do

<Note>
  The role tiers and their hierarchy are enforced today in the backend. The in-product UI for team management is still in development — see [Roadmap](/platform/roadmap). You can already exercise roles via the team API endpoints.
</Note>

| Action | Minimum role |
| :--- | :--- |
| See teams you belong to | Viewer |
| View workflows and runs | Viewer |
| Build, deploy, and rerun workflows | Operator |
| Invite members | Admin |
| Remove members (other than yourself) | Admin |
| Change a member's role | Admin |
| Transfer ownership or delete the team | Owner |

## Inviting members

An admin (or owner) issues an invite from the team API with the invitee's email and a role. Once accepted, the new member appears in `team_members` with the assigned role. Everyone on the team automatically sees the team's connections and workflows, subject to their role.
